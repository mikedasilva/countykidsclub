Nature & Outdoors

Prince Edward County is one of the most beautiful corners of Ontario. Our nature programs bring kids outside to explore, observe, and connect with the natural world all around them.

What kids will experience

Slow down, look closely, and discover how much is happening in the world just outside your door. PEC is an extraordinary classroom.

🥾

Trail walks & exploration

Guided walks through PEC's trails, shorelines, and natural areas — with stops to observe, discover, and ask questions along the way.

🔭

Nature observation & journaling

Learning to notice: birds, insects, plants, weather, water, and seasons. Kids keep their own nature journals to record what they find and see.

🌍

Environmental stewardship

Understanding our impact on the natural world and learning small, real ways kids can care for the places and creatures around them.

Nature Program FAQ

Early questions about our upcoming nature & outdoor programs.

Where will outdoor programs take place?
We're scoping locations across PEC — trails, parks, conservation areas, and shorelines. Specific locations will be shared when programs are announced. Transportation to meeting points will be each family's responsibility.
What should kids wear or bring?
Outdoor programs will require weather-appropriate clothing, sturdy shoes, and a water bottle. A full what-to-bring list will be provided with registration details.
What happens if the weather is bad?
We'll have a clear weather policy in place. Light rain is often part of the outdoor experience — but in cases of severe weather, sessions will be rescheduled or held indoors where possible.
Can I propose a nature program or offer to lead one?
Absolutely. If you're a naturalist, birder, educator, or someone with deep knowledge of PEC's natural environment and a passion for sharing it with kids, reach out at countykidsclub@gmail.com.
